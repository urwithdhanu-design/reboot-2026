# Types of insurance explained

## Property / Home insurance
Protects the structure of your home (buildings) and/or belongings (contents).
Choose Buildings and Contents, Just Buildings, or Just Contents.
Optional extras may include away-from-home cover and high-value item cover.

## Life insurance
Pays a lump sum to beneficiaries if you die during the term.
Often used for mortgages, family income, or inheritance planning.
Term length and cover amount drive the premium.

## Health insurance and Health Plans
Helps pay for private treatment, diagnostics, or wellness benefits.
Our Health Plan partners with Vitality and is aimed at ages 18–79.

## Critical illness
A form of health-related cover that pays if you meet listed serious illness definitions.
Different from income protection and from life insurance.

## Income protection
Pays a regular benefit if you cannot work due to illness or injury.
Designed to replace income, not to pay a single lump sum like critical illness.

## Vehicle insurance (Car and Van)
Legal and financial protection if you cause injury or damage with a vehicle.
Comprehensive is the broadest private car cover; third party is the legal minimum level in quotes.
Van policies can cover personal and business use.

## Pet insurance
Helps with vet fees for accidents and/or illness.
Lifetime cover renews the vet fee limit each year for ongoing conditions (subject to policy terms).

## Travel insurance
Covers medical emergencies abroad, cancellation, baggage, and related trip risks.
Single trip vs annual multi-trip depends how often you travel.

## Parametric insurance
Pays based on objective triggers (weather, index values) rather than a traditional loss adjuster visit.
Useful for speed and transparency when the trigger is clear.

## Which type should I choose?
- Own a house or flat: Home / Property
- Family financial protection: Life and/or Income protection
- Driving: Car or Van
- Dog or cat: Pet
- Going abroad: Travel
- Everyday health benefits: Health Plan
- Serious diagnosis lump sum: Critical illness
